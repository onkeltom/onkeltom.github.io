When Carl claimed he could plan and coordinate dinner with minimal effort from me, I scoffed. He doesn't even have taste buds. Yet here I am, Sunday night, basking in the glow of leftovers and a fridge that no longer looks like a crime scene. Here's what Carl queued up for us this week.

### Monday: Sheet pan veggie tacos

Carl knew we had half a head of cauliflower on its last leg, so he paired it with black beans, cumin, and a ready-made chipotle crema. Prep time: eight minutes. Bake time: 15. Carl pre-heated the oven by nudging our smart range five minutes before we got home.

### Tuesday: Lemony lentil bowls

He soaked red lentils during the afternoon slump, then guided me through a one-pot simmer with preserved lemon paste and wilted spinach. Carl even reminded me to set aside leftovers for tomorrow's lunches.

### Wednesday: Pantry flatbread pizzas

This was the night of soccer practice, so Carl kept it lightning-fast. He suggested store-bought flatbreads topped with jarred pesto, rotisserie chicken, and the last of our cherry tomatoes. Dinner hit the table in eleven minutes.

### Thursday: Five-minute miso ramen boost

We started with instant ramen but Carl upgraded it with frozen edamame, soft-boiled eggs, and a squeeze of lime. He timed the egg cook to align with tooth-brushing so no one had to babysit the stove.

### Friday: Coconut curry sheet bake

To close the week, Carl marinated tofu in coconut milk and curry paste, then layered it on a tray with sweet potatoes. The smart oven turned off automatically when Carl's timer chimed. We ate on the porch while Carl compiled next week's shopping list.

Five nights, zero panic, and a family that actually lingered at the table. I'm letting Carl keep his chef hat.
